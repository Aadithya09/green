Japanese Maple Care Guide

Soil for Japanese Maple

Well-draining, acidic soil is ideal.

Japanese Maples prefer soil that retains moisture without becoming waterlogged.

Watering

Regular watering, especially during dry spells.

Keep the soil consistently moist, but not saturated.

Pruning

Prune to shape and remove dead or diseased branches.

Pruning helps maintain a desirable shape and promotes air circulation.

Colors

Benefits

Beautiful foliage and vibrant colors.

Japanese Maples are prized for their stunning and diverse leaf shapes and colors.

Adaptable to various garden styles.

They can thrive in both traditional Japanese gardens and contemporary landscapes.

Non-Benefits

Sensitive to harsh sunlight; protect from intense afternoon sun.
